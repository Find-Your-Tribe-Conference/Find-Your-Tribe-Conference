    Frequently Asked Questions

    Here you’ll find answers to the questions we get asked the most about the Find Your Tribe conference. If you still can’t find the answer you’re looking for, please get in touch!

    Expand all

    Is there accommodation organised?

    Accommodation is not included in the ticket cost but we have organised an amazing discount with our friends at Tank Stream Hotel! We’ll send you the booking code along with your ticket.

    Is there a dress code?

    Not at all! This is YOUR day. Some may choose to dress up a bit for Mum’s Night Out after the event, but it is totally up to you!

    Will there be exhibitors and items for sale?

    Yes! There will be an exhibit hall with a mix of information and items for sale.

    Will I get to sit next to the people I want to?

    Good question! One of our primary goals throughout the day is to help mums connect. One way we do that is through some organized activities and through some seating arrangements during speakers.

    I am so nervous because I don’t know anyone and am coming by myself. How will I meet people?

    You are NOT alone! Many moms take the leap and come to join us alone. Before the event, we will start a Facebook group to help you all get to know each other a bit. Come with an open mind and heart and you will fit right in!

    Can I bring my kids and family?

    The conference and evening events are for mums only! We are unable to offer childcare, so plan to bring a helper along to hang out with your children while you are connecting, learning, and sharing.

    What is the schedule for the weekend?

    We’ll announce that in the coming weeks! Expect a line up of amazing speakers, awesome workshops, and a whole lot of fun.

    How do I stay up to date on retreat information?

    Check your emails and be sure to join our 2019 Find Your Tribe (T21) Facebook Group once you’re registered for the latest news, tips and information. The group is also a great spot for mums to connect, too!

     
    Penny Robertson

    Penny Robertson is Founder of the Asia Pacific Down Syndrome Association, co-founder of Down Syndrome International, and a regular International keynote speaker. In 1991, Penny was awarded a Member of the Order of Australia for her services to the education of people with intellectual disabilities and in 2014 was nominated as Australian of the year.

     
    Shona Robertson

    Shona Robertson is an artist and published author and spends her time volunteering with her local Federal MP’s office and working at historic Kirra Hill where she is an administrative assistant. Shona is proud of her achievements to date and considers a house, a dog, a paying job, and a fiance the right of any young woman her age.

     
    Catia Malaquias

    Catia is the founder of Starting With Julius, a non profit organisation committed to the equal representation of people with disabilities in advertising, media and education and a co- founder of All Means All – The Australian Alliance for Inclusive Education and the Global Alliance for Disability in Media and Entertainment. She also sits on the boards of Down Syndrome Australia and the Attitude Foundation. Catia is the winner of the 2018 Human Rights Award and the AFR 100 Women of Influence Award for Diversity and Inclusion.

     
    Sandy & Steph

    Co-Founders of “Thrive Tribe & Co”, mums Sandy and Steph connected on the basis that no parent should have to embark on this journey alone and that a parent health needs to be a priority.

    Sandy is a proud Mum to two beautiful kids, Imogen and Cooper. Imogen is on the autism spectrum and has ADHD and anxiety. Sandy herself was diagnosed with Multiple sclerosis in recent years. Sandy's life is dedicated to helping people live their best and healthiest lives. She embodies this notion every single day through her health and business coaching which is completely centred about being your best self and living your life by design.

    Stephanie is a doting Mummy to two little girls, Imogen and Charlotte. Charlotte falls onto the autism spectrum and also has sensory processing issues which can affect her processing of the world around her. Stephanie is just embarking on this journey but is already so passionate about educating women especially on the importance of a balanced and healthy body and mind.

     
    Pieta Manning

    Pieta brings a unique perspective with her experience as a mother of a young person with disability, family advocate, NDIS Coordinator of Support and her consulting work with communities and providers through the NDIS transition.

    Her focus is on building skills and confidence to make the most of the opportunities offered by the individualized funding models now in place through the NDIS. She sees this as important for all stakeholders in our sector and essential to the success of the NDIS moving forward.

    Pieta is a great networker who is always interested in learning and sharing information, exploring collaboration opportunities and making connections that build stronger communities.

     
    Hannah Edwards

    Hannah Edwards is an occupational therapist who has worked in paediatrics for almost 20 years. Hannah has spoken at two Down syndrome NSW Education conferences and supported the development of a Down syndrome group at Camp Jabiru. Hannah is the proud mother of two beautiful children, Flynn and Maiya. Flynn was born in 2007 and diagnosed with Down syndrome shortly after.
